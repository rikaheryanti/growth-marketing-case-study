## Key Insights

### 1. AI search is replacing traditional search behavior
Users are shifting from keyword-based searches to complex, multi-sentence queries. Instead of showing links, AI tools now provide direct recommendations, which changes how products are discovered.

### 2. Visibility depends on your entire digital footprint
AI models pull information from multiple sources including websites, LinkedIn, Reddit, YouTube, and review platforms. This means your overall presence, not just your website, determines how you appear.

### 3. Brand mentions are more important than backlinks
Traditional SEO relied heavily on backlinks. In AI search, what matters more is how often and how consistently your brand is mentioned across credible sources.

### 4. Positioning determines how AI describes your product
AI does not just list companies—it explains them. If your positioning is unclear, AI may categorize your product incorrectly, which directly impacts recommendations.

### 5. Review platforms influence AI outputs
Customer reviews on platforms like G2 and Trustpilot provide strong signals. The language customers use becomes part of how AI understands and recommends your product.

### 6. AEO (AI Engine Optimization) is emerging
Companies need to actively audit how they appear across AI tools using real buyer queries. This is becoming a new layer on top of traditional SEO.

### 7. Early adoption creates competitive advantage
This shift is similar to the early days of Google Ads. Companies that adapt early will gain long-term visibility and advantage.
